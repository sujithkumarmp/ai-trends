**Goal:** To generate defined premium income by betting that the underlying asset price will remain above a key support level (175).
**Strategy:** Selling an Out-of-the-Money (OTM) Put option and buying an even further OTM Put option for risk mitigation.
**Profit Target:** Collecting the net credit premium received at the time of entry.

### Rationale for Selection

1. **Defined Risk:** The primary advantage of a spread is that the maximum potential loss is strictly known upon entry by buying the lower strike option.
2. **Premium Collection:** In a consolidating market, time decay ($\Theta$) typically benefits the seller of options. By selling the high-strike put, we profit from the decay of that premium.
3. **Support Assumption:** By selecting strikes near perceived strong support (e.g., 180), we are capitalizing on the high probability that the underlying asset will experience a dip in volatility rather than a major collapse.

### Risk Management Checklist

* **Stop-Loss:** If the underlying price breaks decisively below 170, the thesis is invalidated, and the position should be reviewed or closed to prevent the maximum loss being realized.
* **Monitoring:** Monitor daily volatility metrics. If implied volatility spikes unexpectedly, the spread's value could change faster than anticipated, requiring a potential hedge adjustment.
