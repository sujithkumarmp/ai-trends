# NVDA Options Analysis
**Date:** 2026-05-02  **Time:** 21-59-46 EDT

**Strategy Selection: Bull Put Credit Spread**

Given the high liquidity and implied volatility visible in the options chain, and with the assumption that the short-term trend might experience a healthy pullback or period of consolidation (selling premium during weak/sideways periods is usually more reliable for income than predicting the peak of a momentum run), a **Bull Put Credit Spread** is selected.

This strategy involves selling a put option (gathering premium) while buying a further out-of-the-money (OTM) put option. This defines the maximum risk (the difference between the strikes minus the premium received) while generating cash flow (the credit).

***

### 🛡️ Risk & Reward Analysis

*   **Maximum Profit:** $0.65 per share ($65 per contract). This profit is realized if the underlying asset's price closes at or above the 190 strike at expiration.
*   **Maximum Loss:** $0.65 per share ($65 per contract). The maximum loss occurs if the underlying asset drops below the 185 strike at expiration.
*   **Breakeven Point:** $190.00 - $0.65 = **$189.35**.
*   **Potential Reward:** The strategy aims for the underlying price to remain above $189.35 before expiration, allowing the premium to decay.

***

### 🎯 Exit Plan & Targets

1.  **Ideal Exit (Profit Taking):** Sell the spread (or roll the position) when the credit received diminishes to half of the initial credit (approx. $0.32). This locks in quick, high-probability profit.
2.  **Profit Target:** Maintain the position for 20-30 days, aiming to let time decay (Theta) diminish the value of the sold put leg faster than the bought put leg.
3.  **Stop Loss:** If the underlying asset price falls sharply below the $180 level, suggesting a major structural breakdown, close the entire spread immediately, accepting a loss to avoid potentially wider losses.

***

### 📝 Action Checklist

1.  **Determine Expiration:** Select an expiration date 30-45 days out (optimizing for Theta decay).
2.  **Execute Trade:** Simultaneously sell 1 contract of the 190 Put and buy 1 contract of the 185 Put.
3.  **Monitor:** Track the underlying asset's price relative to the breakeven point ($189.35).
4.  **Adjust:** If the underlying trend reverses strongly (e.g., gains massive momentum far above $200), consider rolling the spread up and out-of-the-money to maintain a high probability of success.
